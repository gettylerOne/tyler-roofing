What to do, in order.

01

Stay off the roof.

Storm damage is brittle. Cracked shingles look fine and break the second weight goes on them. Document from the ground or with a phone camera. The roof inspection is on us.
02

Walk the perimeter.

Look for: shingle pieces in the yard, granules collected at downspouts, dented gutters or AC fins, broken tree limbs on the roofline, water stains on ceilings or in the attic.
03

Take photos and dates.

Insurers care about timing. Note the storm date, photograph any visible damage, and save weather reports if you can — local news clips, NWS alerts, even neighborhood Facebook posts.
04

Call us before the carrier (or call together).

We'll do a free inspection and tell you straight whether you have a real claim or not. If you do, we coordinate with the adjuster directly. Filing a claim that doesn't pan out can affect your rates — better to know first. A free drone inspection is the fastest way to find out: about 20 minutes on site.
05

Document the claim properly.

If you file, we provide the loss documentation in the format adjusters expect — measurements, photos, line items. Most denials we see are paperwork issues, not damage issues.
06

Schedule the work after approval.

Once the claim is approved, we put you on the schedule, order materials to the spec, and handle the install. You pay your deductible. Carrier pays the rest.

What hail damage
actually looks like.

  • 01
    Bruises on asphalt shingles
    Dark, soft spots where the granule layer is dislodged. The mat underneath is exposed — that's where leaks start.
  • 02
    Missing granules at downspouts
    If the gutter outflow has piles of black sandy material, the shingles are shedding their protective layer.
  • 03
    Dented soft metal
    Vents, flashing, AC condenser fins, gutters — soft metal dents are the easiest tell. Adjusters look here first.
  • 04
    Cracked shingle corners
    A storm-bruised shingle often cracks along its edge within weeks. Visible from a ladder.
